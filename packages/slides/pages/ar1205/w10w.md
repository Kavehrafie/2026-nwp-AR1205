### Neoclassicism
Neoclassicism was a movement in art and architecture that emerged in the mid-18th century as a reaction to the ornate and decorative styles of the Baroque and Rococo periods. It drew inspiration from the art and culture of ancient Greece and Rome, emphasizing simplicity, symmetry, and proportion. Neoclassical artists sought to revive the ideals of classical antiquity, often depicting heroic subjects and moral themes. The movement was characterized by a return to classical forms and techniques, as well as a focus on rationality and order. Neoclassicism had a significant influence on art, architecture, and design throughout the late 18th and early 19th centuries.
- the discovery of Pompeii and Herculaneum in the mid-18th century, which revealed well-preserved examples of ancient art and architecture, inspired artists and architects to emulate classical styles.

### history painting
History painting was considered the highest genre of painting in the hierarchy of genres established by the French Academy. It typically depicted scenes from classical history, mythology, or the Bible, and was meant to convey moral or political messages. History paintings were often large in scale and featured dramatic compositions, with figures arranged in dynamic poses to create a sense of movement and emotion. The genre was popular during the Neoclassical period, as artists sought to revive the ideals of classical antiquity and promote civic virtue through their work.
