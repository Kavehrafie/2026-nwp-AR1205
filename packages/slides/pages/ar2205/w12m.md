#### Historical Context and Consolidation
Fraser traces how the field expanded from the mid-1990s onward through three waves: the growth of museums and curatorial discourse, the financialization of art for the ultra-wealthy, and the proliferation of academic degree programs. This expansion was enabled by neoliberal globalization and, ironically, by the avant-garde’s historical negation of boundaries. Instead of struggling over values, the subfields now exist in a state of mutual dependency (ranging from mutualistic to parasitic), which holds the fragmented field together.

She argues that those seeking to exist "outside" the art field are often already defined by their desire to function within it.
